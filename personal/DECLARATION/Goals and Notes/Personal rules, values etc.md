### Digital Minimalism

So that the digital minimalist looks at technology and ask themselves, what matters to me? How and when? Is the most optimal time for me to use this tech. To move me towards what matters to me. And then? Identify that you are happy with missing out everything else. That you choose to avoid.

1. Automate Your Finances

Save time and stress by setting up automatic processes for your monthly income:

- Hire a fractional CPA
- Audit your monthly debt payments
- Create SOPs for investment outflows (50% savings, 25% robo-advisors, 25% real estate)

2. Conduct a Digital Declutter

Invest in maintaining a clean digital workspace:

- Clean your files/desktop
- Categorize your documents/GDrive
- Delete apps not used in last 30 days
- Leverage the PARA method to organize your projects

3. Practice Time Blocking

Stop waking up with no idea of what to tackle:

- Clear your calendar of useless meetings
- Identify peak energy levels and protect those times
- Build rituals for mindfulness, exercise, and nature

4. Build Mindful Relationships

The value of a network is measured by the depth of relationships, not by the number of relationships.

- Identify your core relationships
- Plan out organic monthly catchups
- Focus on reducing transactionality

5. Adopt the 1 In, 1 Out Rule

With every new addition, learn to remove something from your life.

Apply this to:

- Clothing/Material Goods
- Information inputs (ie. podcasts, newsletters)
- Habits (try not to overcommit yourself,

10-12 is usually the human limit)

1. Set Ambitious Deadlines
Parkinson's Law: Work expands the time we imagine it will take.
For every task you are working on, target half your expected time.
You won't always hit the goal, but you will deliver the project 40% earlier each time.
