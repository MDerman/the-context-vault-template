# 02 - Audience and Positioning

## Audience and Niche

My audience and niche are the same thing: early-stage founders who want to grow their LinkedIn audience and business with inbound leads on LinkedIn.

I include founder-led B2B teams, solopreneurs, agency owners, coaches, course creators, creators with real offers, and B2B operators who need attention to become trust, leads, and revenue.

## One True Fan

This is the bullseye reader. This is the person my content should feel written for.

| Detail | Current Answer |
| --- | --- |
| Age range | 25-40 |
| Role or identity | Early-stage founder, solo founder, founder-led B2B operator, agency owner, coach, or creator with a real offer. |
| Sector or industry | B2B, SaaS, agencies, coaching, services, creator-led businesses. |
| Income or business stage | Building something real; has an offer, product, agency, or business direction, but not enough distribution yet. |
| Family or life context | Ambitious, health-conscious, goal-driven, and trying to build a life with more agency. |
| Location or market | English-speaking LinkedIn market, especially people selling to global B2B audiences. |
| Name or nickname | Founder-led LinkedIn GTM builder. |

## Audience Worldview

Use plain language. Write what they already believe, fear, want, and resent.

| Prompt | My Answer |
| --- | --- |
| What do they value? | High-ROI learning, outside input, independence, useful software, taste, ambition, health, and direct feedback from the market. |
| What do they fear? | Wasting their abilities, staying invisible, building something nobody sees, or losing to teams with more money, credibility, and distribution. |
| What do they want badly? | A larger audience, consistent income, better opportunities, more freedom, and confidence that they have brought something of meaning and value to the world. |
| What do they believe about success? | Hard work is the single way to change a life because it is the only mindset that gives them the opportunity to fight. |
| What do they believe about themselves? | They know they can eventually succeed because they will never stop working. There is no other alternative. |
| What do they think is unfair, broken, or misunderstood? | Generic advice, shallow AI output, confusing tools, ugly internet experiences, and systems that look busy but do not create business. |

## Success Story Framework

### Specific End Outcome

Large social following, consistent income, inbound leads, more trust, better business opportunities, and confidence that the work means something.

### Meaning

It means I am not stuck in a monotonous default path. It means I can attract connections and work I actually want to do. It means I can provide for my family, build something of my own, and bring honour to the people who believed in me.

### Obstacles

The immense amount of dedication, risk, and work that lies ahead, along with habits and beliefs that put me in my own way.

| Problems or Roadblocks | Pain or Emotional Cost | Dreams or External Goals | Desires or Deeper Meaning |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| I do not know what to write or make videos about. | I feel invisible, unheard, and behind everyone else online. | Clear content ideas that sound specific to my business. | Confidence that my ideas matter and can create opportunity. |

## Niche Statement

I am building for founder-led teams that want LinkedIn to move from a posting chore into a GTM motion.

The transformation is practical: clearer ideas, better posts, better conversations, more trust, more leads, and a stronger founder-led brand.

My difference is taste. The product should not create generic AI content. It should help people say what only they can say, then use that content to build relationships.

## Idea Dump

Older emotional center:

> Founders, entrepreneurs and creators starting out on the internet.

> They VALUE outside-input and so enjoy learning when the feel it has a high ROI on their time. They value their health and well-being and are highly goal driven. They seek to be famous and successful by typical definitions - and aren't ashamed of it.

> And FEAR living a life that millions before them have lived, where they haven't pushed themselves or brought something new to the world. They almost want to bring honour to their family like Samurai.

> They crave (GOAL) making an impact or expressing themselves through art in any form, but they understand that in order to avoid becoming a starving artist, you must first expand your abilities through one's own business.

Supporting line to keep:

> If you're here it means you are ambitious, have an open mind, have a desire for greater impact, meaning and purpose in your life, and are willing to take risks. But of course, the greatest risk is taking no risk at all.
