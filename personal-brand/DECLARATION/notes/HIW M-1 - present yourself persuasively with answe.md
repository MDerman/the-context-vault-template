# Dreams:

- **Freedom of Choice**
    
    The ability to live life on their own terms, free from the constraints of a traditional 9-to-5 job or societal expectations.
    
- **Authentic Self-Expression**
    
    A chance to share their unique voice, passions, and creativity with the world without fear of judgment.
    
- **Recognition and Impact**
    
    Being acknowledged and respected for their ideas, while knowing they are positively influencing others' lives.
    
- **A Legacy of Meaning**
    
    The deep satisfaction of leaving a mark on the world—something that feels timeless and valued by others.
    
- **Financial Security**
    
    Earning a steady and reliable income to support their lifestyle and future aspirations without constant worry.
    
- **Personal Confidence**
    
    Developing the self-belief and courage to navigate challenges and seize opportunities with assurance.
    
- **Community and Connection**
    
    Building relationships with like-minded individuals who appreciate their work and support their journey.
    
- **Control Over Time**
    
    The power to allocate their time to what matters most—whether that's work, personal growth, or loved ones.
    
- **Embracing Potential**
    
    Realizing and actualizing their fullest potential, knowing they’ve explored the boundaries of what they’re capable of.
    
- **Living with Purpose**
    
    Feeling a profound sense of alignment and fulfillment in their actions, knowing their work has deeper significance.
    

# Desires

- **Follower Count Growth**
    
    Achieving a substantial and engaged social media following, such as reaching 100K+ followers on their chosen platform.
    
- **Consistent Income Streams**
    
    Generating predictable monthly revenue from social media, personal projects, or freelance work, aiming for $5K–$20K/month.
    
- **Viral Content Success**
    
    Creating posts, videos, or other content that achieve high engagement, like 1M+ views or thousands of likes and shares.
    
- **Brand Partnerships**
    
    Securing collaborations with well-known brands or companies that align with their values and personal brand.
    
- **Published Work**
    
    Writing articles, eBooks, or other content that gets recognized in reputable publications or online spaces.
    
- **Speaking Opportunities**
    
    Being invited to share insights and expertise at events, webinars, or podcasts.
    
- **Professional Network Expansion**
    
    Building connections with industry leaders, influencers, and mentors who can open doors to new opportunities.
    
- **Skill Mastery**
    
    Acquiring advanced knowledge or certifications in areas like content creation, digital marketing, or personal branding.
    
- **Aesthetically Pleasing Platforms**
    
    Developing visually compelling and professional profiles on platforms like Instagram, LinkedIn, or personal websites.
    
- **Awards and Recognitions**
    
    Winning accolades or being featured in "30 Under 30" lists, social media award shows, or industry spotlights.
    

# Problems

talk about these a lot

- **Content Overload**
    
    Struggling to stand out in an oversaturated social media landscape where everyone is competing for attention.
    
- **Inconsistent Branding**
    
    Difficulty defining and maintaining a personal brand that aligns with their values and attracts their ideal audience.
    
- **Lack of Confidence**
    
    Feeling unworthy or doubting their ability to create valuable content or make an impact.
    
- **Time Management Challenges**
    
    Balancing content creation, professional growth, and personal life without feeling overwhelmed.
    
- **Monetization Confusion**
    
    Uncertainty about how to turn their social media presence into a reliable and sustainable income stream.
    
- **Low Engagement Rates**
    
    Experiencing frustration when their posts don’t receive likes, comments, or shares, despite significant effort.
    
- **Fear of Criticism**
    
    Worrying about negative feedback or being judged for their work, which stifles creativity and authenticity.
    
- **Knowledge Gaps**
    
    Lacking the skills or know-how to optimize social media algorithms, create high-quality content, or use analytics effectively.
    
- **Imposter Syndrome**
    
    Comparing themselves to others and feeling like they don’t measure up or deserve success.
    
- **Burnout and Fatigue**
    
    Feeling drained from constantly trying to stay relevant, post regularly, and keep up with trends without a clear strategy.
    

# Pain

- **Feeling Invisible**
    
    The frustration and disheartenment of pouring effort into content creation only to feel overlooked and unacknowledged in a sea of competitors.
    
- **Identity Crisis**
    
    The anxiety and confusion that come from not knowing how to present themselves authentically while still attracting the right audience.
    
- **Crippling Self-Doubt**
    
    The heavy emotional toll of questioning their worth and fearing failure, which paralyzes them from taking bold steps forward.
    
- **Overwhelmed and Stuck**
    
    The sense of being trapped in a never-ending cycle of tasks, unable to make meaningful progress or find balance.
    
- **Fear of Falling Behind**
    
    The panic and stress of seeing peers succeed financially while they struggle to figure out how to monetize their efforts.
    
- **Feeling Unheard**
    
    The deep disappointment of creating heartfelt content that doesn’t resonate with or engage their audience, leaving them questioning their value.
    
- **Fear of Rejection**
    
    The sting of anticipating or experiencing negative feedback, which fuels insecurity and discourages creativity.
    
- **Inadequacy and Frustration**
    
    The demoralizing realization that they lack the knowledge or skills needed to compete and succeed at a high level.
    
- **Shame and Comparison**
    
    The emotional pain of measuring themselves against others’ highlight reels, amplifying feelings of not being good enough.
    
- **Exhaustion and Hopelessness**
    
    The crushing fatigue of trying to keep up with the demands of social media while feeling like their efforts are leading nowhere.

# NOTES

**Defining your core competence**

There are three approaches to writing online:

- **Explorer**: shares what they learn
- **Experimenter**: documents what they do
- **Expert**: shares what they know

For business, you must take on the role of an **expert.** By this, I’m not suggesting you be the best in your niche — you only need to be a few steps ahead because people like to learn from guides, not gurus.

But you must demonstrate expertise that people are willing to pay for.

If you lack expertise, I’ve written an essay on how to go from phase one to phase three [**here**](https://kierandrew.com/how-to-go-from-just-another-creator-to-successful-entrepreneur-by-writing-online/). The path is longer, but possible. You can be an authority in any niche if you’re committed enough to see it through.

Let’s run through 5 questions to help you pick your core competence:

- What do you know better than 95% of people?
- What’s the big problem that you solve for other people?
- Why do friends and family ask you for advice?
- What topics can you talk about for hours on end?
- What transformation has made the most impact in your life?

**Choose your area of highest potential impact.** This leads to the most audience growth. This is a subject you know better than most, that excites you, and can be used in the service of other people.

A: Use AI to grow your audience

Why? Because I was sacrificing my reputation for reach. See, you can hack virality by going super-broad with topics — catering to the masses. But remember this: **chasing followers costs you fans**. Generic content will get you generic results, even if the likes are great. How you grow your audience is much more important than how fast.

Remember this:

**You don’t want to be the writer for everyone. You want to be THE writer for someone.**

And who is that someone?

Your **One True Fan (OTF)**.

A: *Founders, entrepreneurs and creators needing audience growth on the internet.* 

**Everyone is telling themselves a story.**

You and I are running around with a movie in our heads, shaped by our thousands of life experiences: where we grew up. Who our friends were. How our parents behaved. What information we consumed.

From an understanding of other people, this fact gives you immense power (and relief).

Why?

Because you realise **no one is right and no one is wrong**. Religion. Politics. Status. Wealth. Beliefs. Values. These are all just stories we’ve plastered over the fabric of reality.

How can we use this fact as writers?

Simple.

Your One True Fan is telling themselves a story… **and it’s your job to make them the hero**.

Now, in storytelling, there are two things you need to know:

- Intention
- Obstacles

But you must work out *why* it’s important because it’s the deeper meaning that motivates your audience. Ask, **what does this goal mean to them?**

**Don’t just talk about losing 20lbs.** Talk about being a proud father and setting a great example with your body.

**Don’t just talk about scaling a tech company.** Talk about having a well-oiled business that you’re leading with confidence.

**Don’t just talk about landing clients.** Talk about the security and excitement of having a pipeline full of hot leads on your waitlist.

Get. People. Excited.

Now, the end outcome is what you deliver.

You need to ask:

*What’s stopping them?*

You will hear me say this many, many times. Especially if you’re a subscriber of the Magnetic Content Masterclass, where I break down content each week (shameless plug, [**here’s the link**](https://magneticcontent.crd.co/)).

**You must meet your readers where they’re at so that you can lead them where they want to go.**

This is why problem-solving content is so effective. People are motivated to avoid pain much more than achieve pleasure. For example, with Kevin, I speak about his frustration at not getting engagement on his content. Or the disappointment that comes with a lack of audience growth. Or the fact no one is buying his stuff because his copy sucks.

Let’s wrap up module one to get your audience swiping in the right direction.

You have **your** **core competence** — the reason people follow you.

You have **your** **One True Fan** — the person you want to attract.

And you have **their** **end goal** — where you are taking them.
