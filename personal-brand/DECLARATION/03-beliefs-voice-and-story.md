# 03 - Beliefs, Voice, and Story

## Strong Beliefs

What do I believe strongly that my audience needs to hear?

| Belief | Why I Believe It | Who Disagrees |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Creator-led companies will replace established companies that seem impossible to beat. | People follow people, and the internet lets individuals grow massive communities and businesses. | Faceless companies, old-school brand teams, people who think distribution belongs to institutions. |
| If you do not take social media seriously, you are doing your future a disservice. | Visibility is now part of the work. | People who dismiss social media as cringe or unserious. |
| You can always make something refreshing, inspiring and beautiful on the internet. | Everything feels the same only when I stop working hard enough on taste. | People who accept ugly, uninspiring internet work as inevitable. |
| Writing is thinking. | If I outsource all writing, I outsource part of my thinking. | People who want AI to replace their point of view. |
| Average is over. | The quiet trap is accepting mild discontent for too long. | People who say "it is what it is" or "there isn't enough time". |

## Enemies and Tensions

Good brands have tension. These are the ideas, behaviors, norms, and assumptions I am pushing against.

| Enemy or Tension | Why It Matters | Better Alternative |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Cheap AI content slop | It turns smart founders into generic content machines. | Tasteful AI-assisted thinking and writing. |
| Productivity theater | It makes me feel busy without creating published work or real conversations. | Simple systems that create output. |
| Vague personal-brand advice | It sounds nice but does not help me say anything specific. | Specific positioning, proof, audience language, and repeatable content lanes. |
| Blindly accepting advice | It outsources thinking. | Think clearly and keep only what serves the goal. |
| Empty flexing | It performs success instead of building proof. | Build useful things, show proof, and help people move. |
| Average by default | It quietly steals the life I actually want. | Choose a real goal and build toward it with urgency. |

## What I Believe That Most People Do Not

> Your brand is an opinion - it should piss some people off.

> Your goal should not be success, money, fame... it should be the opportunity you have created to work on your own stuff.

> I'm anti-systems when it comes to personal development, but pro systems big time when it comes to business.

> Every founder should be lean and strong.

> Think for yourself.

## What Frustrates Me

> People flexing and 'influencing' masses of young kids.

> people who don't have direction or don't believe in themselves.

> People who say 'it is what it is' or 'there isn't enough time'.

> The fact that most of the internet is ugly, and un-inspiring.

> excessive fucking systems that are dumb - i'm an anti-systems system guy.

## Voice

I want to write refreshing, thought-provoking but highly actionable ideas and stories that leave people empowered to build their business/startup via their own personal brand.

I write like a builder with taste: short, specific, emotionally honest, useful enough to save, allergic to hype, slightly contrarian when it is true, and willing to make strong claims and back them with proof.

## Founder Arc

I was driving home from work after another decent day at my perfect tech job.

Except this average afternoon - that was exactly my signal.

I was the frog being boiled alive.

I was accepting mild-discontent.

I was accepting spending ages after-hours making social media content but having no direction.

I was accepting average.

I had to jump. Now.

The product came from being the market itself. I knew the pain of spending ages after-hours making social media content but having no direction.

My clearer story now is simple: I am building the tool I need, using it in public, and letting the market sharpen it.

## Story Arc

Use this when turning a personal story into content.

| Step | Notes |
| --- | --- |
| The hero | Me, building toward a more useful and creative life. |
| The problem | Comfortable work, mild discontent, scattered content, and no clear direction. |
| The quest for the answer | Learning online, building software, writing, posting, talking to users, and trying to make LinkedIn useful. |
| The failures or false starts | Building too broadly, not being close enough to customers, trying to do too much, and hiding in learning. |
| The insight, solution, or success | Focus on one product, one audience, one specific problem, and build proof in public. |
| The lesson worth sharing | Stop planning. Start doing. Simplicity wins. Build what helps people move. |
