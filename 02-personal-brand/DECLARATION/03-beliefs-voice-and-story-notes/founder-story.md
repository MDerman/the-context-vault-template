# Founder Story

Story material to use in founder essays, About sections, pinned posts, and build-in-public content.

## Origin Moment

> I'm driving home from work after another decent day at my perfect tech job.

> Except this average afternoon - that is exactly my signal.

> I'm the frog being boiled alive.

> I'm accepting mild-discontent.

> I'm accepting spending ages after-hours making social media content but having no direction.

> I'm accepting average.

> I have to jump. Now.

## Builder Arc

> Realize I'm being an idiot and that real learning comes from applying - building.

> I had utter conviction in my success, despite any proof of my ability.

> Same Conviction / Same Focus / Much longer time horizon.

> The decision to fully commit to a single business (startup) was the catalyst.

> I have micro-wins every day.

## Product Origin

> Lots of people ask me why I'm building a LinkedIn marketing app.

> The point is - that my idea sprung out of the problems I experienced - being the market itself!

> Personal brands have to publish that same 'song' over and over for you to see it.

> You keep your brand top-of-mind by repackaging the same ideas over and over.

> So I want to make a system that does that nearly on autopilot, but still with enough creative control for nerds like me.

> I want us solopreneurs to stand a chance against massive social media teams.

## Lessons

> I fear being 'average' and not taking any risks.

> Believing that I could do everything.

> Not being in-touch with real customers enough while building the MVP.

> Being self-conscious.

> perfectionism.
