## Image 7 - CEO Scorecard System, financial metrics

Text-heavy dark Notion page titled **CEO Scorecard System**. It shows the top of a scorecard SOP. A presenter overlay and video progress bar cover parts of the right and bottom.

```text
CEO Scorecard System

Financial Metrics

1. Revenue
   a. The total amount of money earned from selling your product or service.
   b. This is why you are in business. It's important to see what is actually being reported on the financials vs what the sales team is reporting

2. Expenses
   a. The costs incurred to run your business, including things like salaries, rent, and utilities.
   b. Depending on the stage of the business and type of business, expenses should be around 70% of revenue

3. Net Operating Income
   a. The profit your business makes after subtracting all expenses from its revenue. It's a measure of how much money your business is making from its core operations.
   b. The inverse of expenses, this should be around 30% of Revenue. If it's lower then you probably aren't efficient enough in how you deliver your product or service. On the flip side, if you are a lot higher, be careful that this isn't just a flash in the pan and you are able to sustain these margins (especially being mindful of employee turnover).

4. Cash balance
   a. The amount of money your business has in its bank account after accounting for all incoming and outgoing cash transactions.
   b. Vital to the business's ability to operate. Too little cash on hand means very little margin for error or risk. Too high and you could be missing out on opportunities to reinvest in your business. Cash that is just sitting around and not being used is losing value (due to inflation and opportunity cost).
```

The next heading, **Sales Metrics**, begins at the very bottom.
